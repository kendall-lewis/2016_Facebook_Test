About Me

I was born and raised in Cupertino, CA. I went to Humboldt State University for undergrad. Though I officially only changed my major once, I considered and started working towards five different majors: Environmental Science, Dance, Math, Anthropology, and Computer Science. When I graduated I was presented with the Outstanding Student of the Year award for Computer Science - an honor my professors had not told me I would be receiving.

My Education

University of California, Santa Cruz

MS in Computer Engineering

Sept 2013 - Dec 2015

I initially started at UCSC wanting to work on robotics. However, once I started taking courses, I realized that I was more interested in hobbiest-type robotics and working with microcontrollers. I did take several courses in this field though, including:

  • Mechatronics (CMPE 118/218)
  • Models of Robot Manipulation (CMPE 215)

After deciding that robotics wasn't the path I wanted to take, I talked with a lot of professors and fellow students to see what sort of projects they were working on. Through doing this, I was introduced to Natural Language Processing. I took the undergraduate NLP course and found it quite facinating. I switched my focus, got a new advisor, and continued in that direction. Courses I took in this area include:

  • Intro to Natural Language Processing (CMPS 143)
  • Seminar in NLP and Dialogue (CMPM 280Z)
  • Machine Learning (CMPS 242)
  • Computational Models of Discourse (CMPM 245)

My advisor did a lot of work with sarcasm/irony classification from various sources.... Just like any university, there are several courses you are required to take. For me, those courses were:

  • Analysis of Algorithms (CMPS 201)
  • Computer Architecture (CMPE 202)
  • Programming Languages (CMPS 203)

To round out my education/unit requirements, I enrolled in Human-Computer Interaction (CMPE 231). I would actually list this as my favorite CS/CE course I've taken throughout my entire education.
